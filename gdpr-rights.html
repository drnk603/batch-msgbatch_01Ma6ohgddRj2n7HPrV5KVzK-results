Your GDPR Data Subject Rights

At domain, we respect your privacy and are fully committed to upholding your rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). This page explains who you are as a data subject, what rights you hold, and how to exercise them.

What Is GDPR?

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a European Union regulation that governs how organizations collect, process, store, and share personal data. It grants individuals — called data subjects — a comprehensive set of rights over their personal information.

As a customer or visitor of domain ("we," "us," "our"), you are a data subject. We act as the data controller responsible for your personal data. We are also subject to the ePrivacy Directive, which governs cookie and electronic communication practices.

Your Data Subject Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal data that we hold. Each right is subject to certain legal conditions and limitations.

Right of Access

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you, along with information on how and why we process it. This is sometimes called a Subject Access Request (SAR).

Art. 15 GDPR

Right to Rectification

If any personal data we hold about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request that we correct or update it without undue delay.

Art. 16 GDPR

Right to Erasure

Also known as the "right to be forgotten," you may request the deletion of your personal data when it is no longer necessary for its original purpose, or where you have withdrawn consent and no other legal basis applies.

Art. 17 GDPR

Right to Restriction of Processing

You may request that we temporarily halt the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances — for example, while you contest its accuracy or while an objection is being assessed.

Art. 18 GDPR

Right to Data Portability

Where processing is based on your consent or a contract, and carried out by automated means, you may receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, and transmit it to another controller.

Art. 20 GDPR

Right to Object

You have the right to object to our processing of your personal data at any time when it is based on our legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. We will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds.

Art. 21 GDPR

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where we process your personal data based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of any processing carried out before withdrawal.

To withdraw consent, please contact us using the details in the section below, or use the opt-out options provided in the specific service (e.g., unsubscribe links in marketing emails, cookie preference center).

How to Submit a GDPR Request

Exercising your rights is straightforward. Follow the steps below or use our dedicated request form.

  1. 1

    Identify Yourself

    Include your full name and the email address associated with your account or interaction with us. We may ask for additional verification to protect your data.

  2. 2

    Specify Your Request

    Clearly state which right you wish to exercise (e.g., access, erasure, objection) and any relevant details that will help us process your request efficiently.

  3. 3

    Submit via Email or Form

    Send your request to info@domain.com, or complete our Contact Form. Please include “GDPR Request” in the subject line.

  4. 4

    Await Our Response

    We will acknowledge your request promptly and provide a full response within 30 calendar days of receipt. In complex cases, we may extend this by a further two months and will notify you accordingly.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you are unsatisfied with how we have handled your personal data or your GDPR request, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant supervisory authority in your country of residence or the country where the alleged infringement occurred.

In the Russian Federation, the supervisory authority is:

Roskomnadzor (Federal Service for Supervision in the Sphere of Telecom, Information Technologies and Mass Communications)
Website: rkn.gov.ru

For EU-based residents, you may contact the data protection authority in your EU member state. A list of EU supervisory authorities is available on the European Data Protection Board website.

We kindly encourage you to contact us first so we can address your concerns directly and in a timely manner.

ePrivacy Compliance

In addition to GDPR, we comply with the ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC), as amended by Directive 2009/136/EC. This governs our use of cookies, tracking technologies, and electronic communications.

You have the right to control cookie preferences at any time through our Cookie Preference Centre. For full details on how we use cookies, please refer to our Cookie Policy.
